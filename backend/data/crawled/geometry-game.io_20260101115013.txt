Since its launch, the game has continuously evolved through frequent updates focused on gameplay stability, physics accuracy, editor expansion, and overall performance optimization. Dominate Classic Mode with Style Dashmetry’s Classic Mode features twelve main levels with steadily increasing difficulty. Each level introduces new patterns, mechanics, and visual themes, ensuring that every stage feels fresh and rewarding. In each run, players: Navigate through complex obstacles Collect three coins per level Reach the final portal to complete the stage and secure mana orbs The levels are designed around rhythm-based gameplay, combining inventive obstacles with carefully selected background tracks. Players dash through a wide range of environments, including nature-inspired visuals, honeycomb-style arenas, and block-based designs, keeping the pacing dynamic and engaging. Community Levels in the Main Progression Dashmetry also integrates community-created levels directly into the main game experience. A notable example is The Mousai , the first user-created level promoted to main-level status. Rated at Normal difficulty (3 stars) , it quickly became a fan favorite thanks to its neon visuals, rhythmic flow, and cleverly timed jumps. This approach highlights Dashmetry’s commitment to player creativity and community recognition. Explore More Content in Classic Mode Beyond the main levels, Dashmetry offers multiple level folders that expand gameplay variety: Recents – Discover the latest uploaded levels Featured – Developer-approved maps with polished design Popular – Community-loved levels with strong positive feedback Trending – Levels gaining rapid attention and play counts Favorites – Save and replay your preferred maps instantly These folders ensure a steady stream of new challenges and replay value. Dashmetry Level Editor – Create Your Own Challenges Dashmetry empowers players with a powerful built-in level editor , allowing anyone to design, test, and share custom levels. Players can access the editor from the main menu or directly within Classic Mode. The creation flow includes: Naming the level Setting difficulty and game mode Selecting music and level metadata Inside the editor, creators can place platforms, spikes, saws, portals, and triggers to build unique challenges. Visual customization tools allow decoration, background styling, object rotation, flipping, blending, and invisible triggers. Each level can be tested repeatedly to fine-tune jumps, rhythm alignment, and difficulty balance before publishing it to the community. With enough creativity, players can design anything from beginner-friendly maps to high-speed, rhythm-perfect challenges. Game Modes – Three Ways to Dash Forward Dashmetry offers three distinct modes , each testing different skills: Classic Mode – Complete 12 structured levels ranging from 3-star to 8-star difficulty Endless Mode – Dash through an infinite course and survive as long as possible to set high scores Race Mode – Compete against other players in real-time, racing to the final portal while avoiding hazards Each mode provides a unique pacing and challenge style, ensuring long-term replayability. Accounts, Progression, and Player Stats Dashmetry supports a free account system that allows players to securely save progress. By creating an account through the profile menu, players can store: Mana orbs and coins Completed levels Achievements and progression The system also tracks detailed statistics such as total jumps, attempts, playtime, daily logins, and overall performance across all game modes.
