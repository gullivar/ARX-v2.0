Roxy Music
Love Is The Drug (50th Anniversary Edition)
Universal
Half a century after its original release, 'Love Is The Drug' finally gets its own personal 12", a fitting tribute to one of rock's most alluring and influential singles. This special edition brings together the definitive original mix, Greg Wilson's extended edit, Todd Terje's cosmic 'Disco Dub' and the original B-side, 'Sultanesque'. The song's timeless magnetism, within its sleek, seductive pulse, kicked off the band's more mature and sophisticated style as they embraced dance music in the form of disco. As Roxy Music's first major international hit, John Gustafson's unforgettable bassline and Bryan Ferry's effortlessly debonair delivery, led the way. Provocative and reserved in equal measure. Greg Wilson's re-edit reimagines the song's potential had the 12" format existed in 1975, making it into a late-night club odyssey perfect for Roxy/Bowie nights that inspired the new wave movement.

Philadelphia's soul studios in the early 70s were a world of their own and Blue Magic were among the few groups who could move through that landscape with genuine ease.
