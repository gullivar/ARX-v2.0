Different ATS systems flag a few resume formatting issues that can lead to ATS parsing errors, thus making some resume templates not ATS-friendly. Here's how our builder stacks up against each point, and why we're confident in our ATS-readiness. Enhancv resumes do not include graphics, photos, or word art that are not ATS-Friendly - Our templates use decorative icons and images as visual elements only - never as text containers. All text remains selectable and lives in the text layer. That means parsers simply skip the iconography without losing any data. Enhancv resumes are not uploaded as an image (flattened PDFs) - We export proper PDFs with a full text layer, not rasterized images. You can copy-paste any text from our resumes — exactly what parsers read. Flattened PDFs (a known parsing failure) don't occur in our generation pipeline. Enhancv resumes do not use complex designs with tables, headers, and footers - We use consistent section grouping and rely on HTML-to-PDF text flow - not embedded Word tables or footers. Only one legacy template uses a minimal table structure, and even that's fully textual, not graphic. Contact info sits in the main body, not headers/footers, so it's accessible to parsers. Enhancv resumes do have a columned layout, however, our column structure isolates sections' content - for example, Experience on the left, Skills/Education on the right. We don't split one section across both columns, so reading order (left to right, top to bottom) remains coherent. Enhancv resumes have clear sections or and consistent formatting - Each section in Enhancv templates is clearly labeled ("Experience," "Education," "Skills") and uses a consistent heading style. These titles match parser dictionaries used by Sovren and RChilli, improving section classification. Every known risk category listed by major ATS systems is either already resolved by design or confined to user input, not builder output. Our PDF exports meet modern parsing standards — selectable text, consistent structure, clear labeling, and no flattened layers. This positions us confidently as ATS-compatible by design, not just by workaround.
