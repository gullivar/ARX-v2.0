WHAT ARE THE TERMS USED IN SATTA MATKA GAMES ?

Single Ank - Any number between 0 to 9
Jodi - Any two digit number between 00 and 99
Pana/Patti/Panel - A trail of three cards in a single draw.
Open Panna - The three cards drawn during the opening part.
Close Panna - The three cards drawn during the closing part.
Single Patti - Three distinct digits. e.g. 123
Double Patti - Two similar digits. e.g. 233
Triple Patti - Three similar digits. e.g. 222

HOW DID SATTA MATKA GAME START (HISTORY OF MATKA GAMES)

The matka game originated in the 1950s when people used to place bets on the opening and closing rates of cotton that were transmitted to the Bombay Cotton Exchange from the New York Cotton Exchange, via teleprinters.

In 1961, the New York Cotton Exchange stopped the practice, which caused the gamblers to look for alternative ways to keep the Satta matka business alive. Then it was proposed that People could simply bet on randomly generated numbers and that was the beginning of present day number based satta matka.
