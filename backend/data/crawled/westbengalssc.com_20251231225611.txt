Who  can apply for 2nd SLST(AT), 2025 Examination ? Any citizen of India only may apply for the post of Teachers i.e., Assistant Teachers (Classes IX & X) and Assistant Teachers (Classes XI & XII) in Government Aided / Sponsored Secondary / Higher Secondary Schools. Eligible candidates with the Minimum qualification laid down in the Rules, 2025 may apply online only (and by no other mode) through the official website of The West Bengal School Service Commission (i.e., http://www.westbengalssc.com ). What will be the whole process for 2nd SLST (AT), 2025 Examination? The selection process consists of the following stages: OMR based Written test Evaluation of educational qualification and prior teaching experience. Oral interview. Lecture demonstration. Please provide some details of Written Examination. The written examination of 2nd SLST (AT), 2025 have: Multiple Choice Question (MCQ)-based written Examination. The paper consists of 60 questions of 1 mark each, with a duration of 1 hour 30 mins. For PH candidates 20 minutes of additional time will be provided. No negative markings will be applicable. Candidates can retain a duplicate copy of OMR they have written. Model answers will be uploaded as per Rule, 2025. What is the total Score Calculation for IX-X and XI-XII of 2nd SLST (AT), 2025 Examination ? Selection to the post of Assistant Teacher for Classes IX-X and XI-XII shall be made on the basis of marks obtained in written examination, educational qualification, prior teaching experience, oral interview and lecture demonstration, determined by the Central Commission, in the following manner: Written Examination (OMR-based) :  60 marks. Academics. Educational Qualification :  10 marks. [Based on marks of Graduation or Post Graduation in the subject for which applied, whichever is higher( Only for IX-X), according to the following formula: Percentage    obtained Merit    Score 60% and above 10 marks Below 60% to 50% 8 marks Below 50% 6 marks No rounding off of percentage is allowed ] Prior Teaching Experience :  10 marks. [At the secondary level in a government or government aided or government sponsored school, calculated on the basis of 2 marks for each year of service in a substantive post.
