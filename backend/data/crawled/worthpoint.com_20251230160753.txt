KART RACING - RACES
Kart racing began around 1956 in the United States. It is a type of racing sport in which drivers race in a purpose-built vehicle called a kart—a small, lightweight car with a tubular frame. Kart racing tests a driver’s speed and handling. A driver must be the first to cross the finish line to win a race. By the 1960s, kart racing had spread from the United States to Europe, where regular races were held yearly.
