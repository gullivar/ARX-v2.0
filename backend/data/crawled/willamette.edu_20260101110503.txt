Greener Factories. Faster Ice Cream. Smarter Systems. That’s Industrial Engineering. Industrial engineering at Willamette is all about making people, technology, and systems play nicely together so you can design smarter processes, cut waste, and boost safety. It’s the same mindset Willamette alum Jadrian Teunissen BS’21 uses at Tillamook, where Python and modeling help turn complex dairy operations into real energy savings (and better ice cream). Engineering Meets Data. And People. And Policy. Turn Everyday Headaches Into Elegant Solutions Industrial engineering is one of the most in-demand fields in the country, with low unemployment and strong starting salaries. Pair that with Willamette’s small classes and deep mentoring, and you’ve got a future-proof degree with real-world impact. Squirrels, Mill Stream Birthday Dunks, and Scholarships Willamette’s priority application deadline is January 15. Submit your application and you’ll get your admission decision by mid-February. There’s no application fee, we’re test-optional, and every applicant gets automatic consideration for merit scholarships. One of the Best Career Centers in the Nation is Right Here in Salem At Willamette, career development is central to the student experience. Our new $2 million Career Center puts job-readiness into everyday campus life. Real advising. Real connections and networking. Real results. Our students are landing internships early, building skills, and walking into graduation with job offers in hand. Career Help Shouldn’t Feel Like an Afterthought So We Put Ours in the Center of It All From day one, students have access to career coaches, employer meetups, and private interview rooms. Not after graduation. Not their junior year. Now. Sixty percent of our students complete internships and nearly all engage with career services.
