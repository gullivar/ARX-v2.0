ICANN’s Commitment to the Africa Community
ICANN has a long-standing and valued relationship with stakeholders across Africa that began more than two decades ago. On 8 April 2005, ICANN recognized AFRINIC as the Regional Internet Registry to manage, distribute, and register Internet number resources across the continent in alignment with its community-developed policies. Over the following years, ICANN has expanded its engagement in the region, invested in infrastructure, and developed local technical capacity to foster a more secure, resilient, and accessible Internet.

ICANN at the U.N.: WSIS+20 and the Role of Collaboration
On 17 December, ICANN President and CEO Kurtis Lindqvist addressed the United Nation (U.N.) General Assembly during its High-Level Meeting on the overall review of the implementation of the outcomes of WSIS+20. Kurtis, and others in the technical community, were invited to share their views with the U.N. member states. Click the title to learn more and see highlights.

Name Collision IPv6 Research Study
ICANN org seeks input on the Controlled Interruption IPv6 Research Study Summary Report, which proposes the use of ::ffff:127.0.53.53 in the AAAA record for future controlled interruption activities.
Closes: 22 December 2025
String Similarity Evaluation Guidelines for New gTLD Program: 2026 Round
ICANN org is seeking community input on the updated String Similarity Evaluation (SSE) Guidelines, which are intended to be used by the SSE panel to conduct an evaluation of the applied-for strings in the New gTLD Program: 2026 Round.
