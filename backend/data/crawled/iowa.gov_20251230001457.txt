Symbols of Iowa
State Bird: Goldfinch

In 1933 the legislature named the eastern goldfinch, also called American goldfinch or wild canary as the state bird.

State Tree: Oak

The oak was named as the official state tree in 1961 for its source of food and shelter for Iowa wildlife.

State Quarter: Education

Minted in 2004, the quarter features Grant Wood's painting Arbor Day, symbolizing Iowa's "Foundation in Education."

Iowa State Flag

Designed by Dixie Cornell Gebhardt of Knoxville and adopted in 1921. It features Iowa's state motto and an eagle.
