How it works.
We catch most spam based on the behavior of the spammer rather than the content of the message. Spammers are on the run these days operating mostly through virus infected spam bots. Spam bots send spam differently that normal email servers. They try to sneak spam in the back door. They don't issue QUIT commands to close the connection. They often use email addresses mined from web sites and do dictionary attacks. These behaviors and others allow use to know a message is spam without having to look at the content of the message.

The two level spam filtering makes this service unique. Messages are classified on a points system using Spam Assassin. Messages that score very high are considered high spam and they are blocked. Messages that score between 5 points and 15 are considered low spam. These messages are flagged with special message headers indicating that they are "probably spam" and are passed on to your server. You can create filter rules to see these messages and route them to a spam folder.

The reason we do it this way is because no spam filter is perfect. But we deal with the fact that messages are missed or wrongly identified as spam in a way that helps to ensure that you don't lose any email.

If a message were falsely marked as spam - it would fall into the low spam classification. But with the high scoring spam removed completely - these false positives are far easier to find because they are in a much smaller pile of messages that are spam. This way the message is still delivered to you but because we get rid of the high scoring spam - there is far less spam reaching your server and it makes these false positives easy to find.

Although we still recommend that end users use anti-virus software on their workstations and laptop computers - we provide a second level of defense - and a better defense against viruses. And an even greater lag time till end users download new virus definitions which might be as little as once a week. This creates a window of exposure where your end users are unprotected.

We use a server side virus filter called ClamAV. Our definitions are updated hourly so we are more likely to stop those new viruses faster than workstation installed software will. Our system gives your users far stronger protection preventing then from being infected and resulting in downtime - loss of data - and costly repairs.
